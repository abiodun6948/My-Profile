Education

  • Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Electrical Engineering
    Universiti Teknologi Malaysia.
    My Ph.D. research is focused on the development of model predictive control (MPC) strategy with Internet of things (IoT) monitoring for optimal irrigation scheduling decisions which was tested experimentally for drip irrigation management. I also developed data-driven models which are able to predict the soil moisture content while accounting for the time-varying nature of the soil, plant, and weather nexus. I further designed a Laguerre Network-based MPC to adaptively control drip irrigation systems, while minimizing the computational complexity associated with the conventional MPC. This has been shown, through simulations, to achieve a 40 % reduction in irrigation water use when benchmarked against a linear quadratic regulator (LQR). Finally, I also applied Kalman filter-PID to predictively control the water supply depth (dh) while maintaining the available water content within the allowable constraints for fibrous capillary irrigation.
    2021 2018
